London

About the Destination

London, the capital of England and the United Kingdom, is a 21st-century city with history stretching back to Roman times. At its center stand the imposing Houses of Parliament, the iconic ‘Big Ben’ clock tower, and Westminster Abbey, the site of British royal coronations.

The city is globally famous for its diverse culture and historic landmarks. Across the Thames River, the London Eye observation wheel provides panoramic views of the South Bank cultural complex, and the entire city. Visitors can explore the world-class collections of the British Museum or enjoy a royal stroll through St. James's Park toward Buckingham Palace.

Historical Significance

From the medieval Tower of London to the grandeur of the British Empire, London has been a pivotal player in world history for centuries. Today, it remains a global powerhouse of finance, fashion, and the arts, while carefully preserving its rich heritage through its many protected monuments and traditional ceremonies like the Changing of the Guard.

Fast Facts

  • Language: English
  • Currency: British Pound (£)
  • Transport: The Underground (The Tube), Red Buses
